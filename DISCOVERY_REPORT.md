## 6. Project Management Process & Tools

### 6.1 Weekly PM Meeting Structure

**Source:** `APRIL/2026 Project Managment Meeting - Working Agenda.docx`

**Attendees:** April Lewis (Leader), Dustin Bolgrihn, John Corwin, Chris Fletcher, Nick Patel, David Onachilla

**Meeting runs every Monday at 1:30 PM** with four main agenda sections:

1. **Engineering & Drawings** — Certified drawings, change orders, job release readiness (drawings printed, BOMs, burn files, build plan, materials received)
2. **Jobs in Production** — RAG (Red/Amber/Green) review of all jobs vs. committed dates; customer inspections in next 2 weeks; India shipping schedule
3. **Shipping Schedule** — 3-week lookahead; super loads (14'+ diameter) need 4–6 weeks advance trucking arrangements
4. **Post-Shipment Close-Out** — As-built drawings, QC package, customer feedback (within 2 weeks of shipment)

### 6.2 Project Lifecycle Process

**Source:** `APRIL/Project Process.xlsx`

Complete order-to-ship workflow:
1. **Kick-Off Meeting** (PO Received) → Review T&Ls, scope, schedule → Intake prep (Job History, Entry Form, Design Form, UDR1 for ASME) → JobBoss entry → Add to Production Schedule → Order long-lead items → Engineering calculations & drawings → Send preliminary schedule to customer
2. **Drawing Approval Cycle** → Review meeting → Send to customer → Receive comments → Edit → Repeat until certified
3. **Drawing Certified** → Invoice released → BOM finalized → ITP created → Build plan → Burn files → Purchase remaining items
4. **Release to Shop** → Customer updates → Schedule communication → Photo updates → Meetings
5. **Prep for Shipping** → Documentation checklists → Verify delivery date → Shipping quotes → Coordinate with Ops and Sales
6. **Shipping Day** → Check loose items/spares → Photographs

## 9. Key Findings & Observations

### Strengths
1. **Strong strategic framework** — Clear vision, measurable objectives, assigned champions, and regular tracking cadence
2. **Significant growth trajectory** — 2025 job count 5x over 2024; targeting $20M in 2026 orders
3. **Efficiency above target** — YTD overall shop efficiency at 94% vs. 93% goal
4. **Structured PM process** — Weekly meetings, RAG reviews, standardized lifecycle, comprehensive tracker
5. **India expansion** underway for cost-competitive capacity

### Risks & Concerns
1. **Capacity strain** — Massive volume increase with only ~25% headcount growth planned; production schedule shows tight/negative capacity weeks
2. **Hydro and Shipping efficiency gaps** — 62% and 56% YTD respectively, dragging overall numbers
3. **At-Risk jobs** — Kimberly-Clark 25-013 at risk 3 days before ship date; Coastal Tech on hold
4. **Liquidated Damages exposure** — Multiple SNF jobs carry LDs; schedule slippage = financial penalties
5. **Uncertified drawings** — 25+ jobs awaiting certification including large Andritz dissolving tank and multiple SNF jobs
6. **India quality ramp** — First-pass approval rate target of >90% suggests current quality gap; ISO-9001 not yet achieved
7. **Key positions unfilled** — Finishing Area Supervisor, additional QC Inspector, additional welders needed
8. **Multiple process dependencies** — Manual spreadsheet-based tracking across many files creates risk of data inconsistency and missed handoffs

### Automation Opportunities

2. **Order-to-Shop Release workflow** — Identified as a strategic initiative
3. **Production scheduling** — Currently manual Excel; significant opportunity for automation
