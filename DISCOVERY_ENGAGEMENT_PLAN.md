# Savannah Tank & Equipment — 3-Week Discovery Engagement Plan
**Client:** Savannah Tank & Equipment (ST&E)
**Engagement Type:** Office Automation Discovery
**Duration:** 3 Weeks
**Start Date:** _TBD_
**Prepared by:** Kelly Arseneau
**Date Prepared:** March 18, 2026

---

## Purpose

Savannah Tank & Equipment has grown from 74 jobs in 2024 to 360+ jobs in 2025, with a 2026 target of $20M in new orders and $14M+ in revenue. The office staff of 8 people is running the same manual, spreadsheet-driven processes that worked at one-fifth of the current volume. **The purpose of this engagement is to identify, prioritize, and document the highest-impact automation opportunities for ST&E's office operations.**

This is NOT an implementation engagement. The deliverable is a prioritized automation roadmap with enough detail to estimate, scope, and execute.

## Interview Guides by Role

### David Onachilla — General Manager / Strategy
**Focus:** Big picture vision, budget constraints, appetite for change

1. What's keeping you up at night with this growth?
2. Where do you see the biggest bottleneck — shop floor or office?
3. If you could wave a magic wand and fix one office process tomorrow, what would it be?
4. What's your budget range / appetite for automation investment in 2026?
5. Are there processes that worked fine at 74 jobs/year that are now breaking?
6. How do you feel about JobBOSS? Is it the long-term system or are you considering alternatives?
7. What's the OTTO estimating tool status — who's building it, what stack, when is it expected to be in daily use?
8. When you think about adding headcount vs. automating — what's your preference?
9. What does "success" look like 12 months from now for the office team?
10. Are there any compliance, security, or IT constraints we should know about?

### April Lewis — Project Manager
**Focus:** The central hub of all job tracking. Her processes ARE the automation opportunity.

1. Walk me through your typical Monday — from the moment you sit down to when you leave. What do you touch?

3. Where does your data come from? (Email? Verbal? JobBOSS? Shop floor?)

13. The RTR (Release to Shop Readiness) checklist in your tracker — is that actually enforced, or do jobs sometimes hit the floor without all items checked?

## Automation Opportunity Scoring Framework

Each opportunity will be scored on a 1-5 scale across four dimensions:

| Dimension | What It Measures | Weight |
|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| **Time Savings** | Hours/week freed up across all affected roles | 30% |
| **Error Reduction** | Risk of missed dates, wrong data, dropped handoffs | 25% |
| **Scalability Impact** | Will this break worse at 500+ jobs/year? | 25% |
| **Implementation Feasibility** | Can this be done with existing systems (JobBOSS + tools)? | 20% |

**Score = (Time × 0.30) + (Error × 0.25) + (Scale × 0.25) + (Feasibility × 0.20)**

## Key Context from Pre-Discovery Document Review

The following was identified from documents already provided and will be validated during interviews:

1. **JobBOSS is the ERP** — SDK and data dictionary are in hand, meaning API integration is technically feasible
2. **April's PM Tracker is the operational nerve center** — 6 sheets, 360+ rows, manually maintained
3. **The Monday PM meeting drives the week** — all status updates, risk flags, and decisions flow from this
4. **Standard ship date formula exists:** Fab (14 days) + Testing (7) + Painting (7) + Cleaning (7) + Shipping Prep (7) = 42 days baseline
5. **39 employees total** — 31 shop, 8 office — adding automation is cheaper and faster than adding headcount
6. **India operations add complexity** — engineering work split across time zones with quality gaps
7. **OTTO (cost estimation tool) is already in development** — validates appetite for automation
8. **Liquidated Damages on SNF jobs** — schedule accuracy has direct financial consequences
